# Pitching to Hg Catalyst

**Hackathon Briefing — What they believe, what they build, what they want to see**

---

## Who you are pitching to

Hg is a London-based PE firm with $110B+ AUM and a portfolio of 60+ B2B software businesses worth $195B. Top 3 in Europe, top 10 globally. Hg Catalyst is their AI incubator (launched November 2025) with 80+ AI engineers, PMs, and designers embedded directly into portfolio companies to ship production-grade agentic AI products.

*Their fintech focus is narrow and specific: software and data businesses underpinning asset management, wealth management, and capital markets. Plus deep adjacencies in accounting, treasury, audit, risk, and compliance.*

---

## The single most important thing to understand

> **AI models are no longer a moat — they are the new baseline.**
>
> Every team at the hackathon has access to the same frontier models. Hg knows this. They will not be impressed that you used Claude or GPT. Differentiation comes from domain depth, proprietary data, and workflow integration. The question they are asking is: *what did you do with that access that nobody else could?*

---

## The five beliefs that drive their evaluation

### 1. Systems of Record → Systems of Action

Their flagship framing. Traditional B2B software stores data and tracks activity (Brockman calls it "automating filing cabinets"). Agentic AI lets incumbent software become a *system of action* — software that executes parts of the workflow itself, with humans directing and overseeing.

- **Implication:** Don't build a dashboard or a chatbot. Build an agent that does the work and presents the result for approval.

### 2. Narrow workflows beat broad copilots

Catalyst explicitly defines narrow workflows with clear success metrics, fast release cycles, and reusable patterns. They are skeptical of "AI assistant for [whole industry]" framings.

- **Implication:** Pick one bounded task. Do it end-to-end. Show the success metric.

### 3. Mission-critical, regulated, proprietary

They invest in companies that have spent years mastering a difficult workflow with complex rules, proprietary data, and deep up/downstream integrations. Regulation is not a barrier; it is the moat.

- **Implication:** Lean into compliance, audit trails, and regulatory complexity. Don't apologise for it — make it the product.

### 4. Demo polish ≠ customer value

Lloyd Hilton, Head of Catalyst: there is a huge difference between AI that demos well and AI products that deliver deep customer value. They have publicly drawn this line.

- **Implication:** Spend less time on the front-end animation and more time on the actual workflow logic, data, and evaluation.

### 5. Real ROI, measured in production

Their published bar: 40%+ new logo bookings uplift in year one for top products, 10%+ of bookings from AI features across portfolio companies. They benchmark against P&L impact, not user delight.

- **Implication:** Quantify your ROI claim. "Saves X hours per fund per quarter" beats "reduces friction."

## What to do — and what to avoid

| ✅ What they want to see | ❌ What will lose them |
|---|---|
| A bounded, end-to-end workflow with one clear user persona | "AI assistant for finance" — too broad |
| An agent that takes action with a human approval gate | Consumer fintech (budgeting apps, neobanks, retail trading) |
| Compliance-by-design — explainability, audit trail, data inside the perimeter | A wrapper around an LLM with no domain depth |
| A proprietary or hard-to-replicate data angle (rule sets, integrations, domain logic) | A chatbot that only answers questions |
| A measurable ROI claim (hours saved, error rate, time-to-market) | Crypto / Web3 framings without a B2B anchor |
| A target user that exists today inside an incumbent — not a future market | "We will disrupt the incumbents" — Hg backs incumbents who become AI-first |
| Evals — show how you would test this in production | Vague benefits like "reduces friction" or "improves UX" |
| Built on top of how the work actually gets done today | Standalone tool that doesn't plug into an existing workflow |

## Speak their language

Borrow these phrases in your pitch. They signal that you understand the thesis without overclaiming.

- "System of action, not system of record"
- "Agentic workflow with human oversight"
- "Bounded task, executed end-to-end"
- "Embedded in the existing workflow"
- "Compliance by design — data stays inside the perimeter"
- "Built on decades of domain knowledge and proprietary data"
- "Re-founding [the workflow] as AI-first"
- "Production-grade, not a pilot"

---

## Pre-pitch self-check

Before you go on stage, your project should answer all eight of these in one sentence each:

1. **Who is the user?** *(One specific role inside one specific kind of company.)*
2. **What is the bounded workflow?** *(One sentence, with a verb.)*
3. **What action does the agent take?** *(Not "helps with" — what does it actually do?)*
4. **Where does the human approve?** *(Show the gate.)*
5. **What is the proprietary or domain edge?** *(Why can't a smart team rebuild this in two weeks with the same model?)*
6. **How does it fit into existing software?** *(API, embedded, integration.)*
7. **What is the ROI number?** *(Quantified, even if estimated.)*
8. **How would you evaluate quality in production?** *(Evals, golden datasets, human review loop.)*
